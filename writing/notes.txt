Our initial experiments mapping the target class to uniform noise (Untargeted Amnesia) failed for the cGAN, resulting in severe mode collapse when using adversarial loss, and a stubborn resistance to unlearning when using L1 loss. We discovered that implementing Targeted Amnesia—explicitly mapping the latent manifold of class '0' to the latent manifold of class '8'—drastically stabilized the generator and achieved a Forgetting Precision near 0.0 without catastrophic forgetting.


During our hyperparameter optimization, we observed that applying standard EWC penalties (λ≥1.0) to complex models like Normalizing Flows and Autoregressive networks resulted in 'hyper-memory'—a complete resistance to unlearning. This indicates that their Fisher Information Matrices operate on vastly different magnitude scales than the VAE. By reducing λ by three orders of magnitude (λ∈[0.001,0.1]), we successfully unfroze the weights and achieved a proper Pareto trade-off. Similarly, we found that cGANs require significantly longer exposure to the amnesiac objective (25+ epochs) alongside deeply suppressed replay constraints (γ≤0.05) to overcome their inherent latent entanglement and avoid adversarial mode collapse.
